COVID-19 Scenario of Bangladesh Compared to the World (April 2020 Snapshot)
February 15, 2026

A comprehensive geospatial visualization comparing the global spread of COVID-19 with the situation in Bangladesh and Dhaka City during the early pandemic phase.

Project Background

In April 2020, I collaborated with GroupMappers and MORU Tropical Health Network to create timely, visually impactful geospatial communication tools. The map was designed to help decision-makers understand Bangladesh's case numbers compared globally, the spatial distribution of cases within Bangladesh, and the intense concentration in Dhaka City.

Key Design Choices
  • Proportional symbol map using graduated yellow-to-red circles
  • Focused inset maps for Europe, Dhaka City, and Bangladesh
  • Color-blind friendly classification with clear size hierarchy
  • Contextual comparison boxes showing global statistics
  • Strong call-to-action messaging: "Stay Home! Stay Safe!"

The Spark of Demographics: Mapping Population Trends in Bangladesh
February 20, 2026

A visually striking population density map of Bangladesh using 2022 BBS data, highlighting extreme urban concentration around Dhaka.

Project Overview

This map visualizes Bangladesh's population distribution at the union/upazila level using BBS 2022 data, illustrating the overwhelming dominance of Dhaka, high population density along major river corridors, and contrast between densely populated eastern/central regions vs. sparser northern areas.

Cartographic Highlights
  • Proportional symbol mapping with red glowing circles sized by population
  • Dark background + glowing red points — a "spark / heat" effect
  • Strong visual hierarchy: Dhaka stands out dramatically
  • Rivers shown in light blue to emphasize physical geography influence

Full population distribution map – BBS 2022 data

Tools Used

ArcGIS Pro / ArcMap · Adobe Illustrator · Excel / Google Sheets

Insight: This map style (dark + glowing points) has proven very effective for social media sharing and public presentations.
